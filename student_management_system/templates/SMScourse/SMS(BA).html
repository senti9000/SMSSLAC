BACHELOR OF SCIENCE IN BUSINESS ADMINISTRATION MAJOR IN HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT MANAGEMENT (HRDM)

The Bachelor of Science in Business Administration Major in Human Resource Development Management (HRDM) program is designed to equip students with the essential skills and knowledge required for effective human resource management in various organizational settings. This program emphasizes both theoretical frameworks and practical applications, focusing on areas such as talent acquisition, employee development, performance management, and organizational behavior. Students gain hands-on experience through internships, case studies, and capstone projects that align with industry standards.

Key Areas of Study:

Principles of Management and Organizational Behavior

Talent Acquisition and Recruitment Strategies

Employee Training and Development

Performance Management and Appraisal Systems

Compensation and Benefits Management

Labor Relations and Employment Law

Diversity and Inclusion in the Workplace

Strategic Human Resource Management

Primary Job Role:

Human Resource Manager: Oversees HR functions, including recruitment, training, and employee relations, ensuring alignment with organizational goals and compliance with labor laws.

Talent Acquisition Specialist: Focuses on sourcing, interviewing, and hiring candidates to meet the staffing needs of the organization, utilizing various recruitment strategies and tools.

Training and Development Coordinator: Designs and implements training programs to enhance employee skills and knowledge, fostering professional growth and organizational effectiveness.

Compensation and Benefits Analyst: Analyzes and develops compensation structures and benefits programs, ensuring competitiveness and compliance with regulations.

Employee Relations Specialist: Manages employee relations issues, mediating conflicts and fostering a positive workplace culture through effective communication and conflict resolution strategies.

Organizational Development Consultant: Works with organizations to improve processes, enhance employee engagement, and implement change management initiatives.

HR Data Analyst: Utilizes data analytics to assess HR metrics, providing insights that inform strategic decision-making and improve HR practices.

Diversity and Inclusion Officer: Develops and implements strategies to promote diversity and inclusion within the organization, ensuring equitable practices and a supportive work environment.

BACHELOR OF SCIENCE IN BUSINESS ADMINISTRATION MAJOR IN MARKETING MANAGEMENT (MM)

The Bachelor of Science in Business Administration Major in Marketing Management (MM) program is designed to provide students with a comprehensive understanding of marketing principles and practices. This program equips students with the skills and knowledge necessary to develop effective marketing strategies, understand consumer behavior, and analyze market trends. Emphasizing both theoretical concepts and practical applications, students engage in hands-on experiences through internships, projects, and case studies that align with industry standards.

Key Areas of Study:

Marketing Principles and Strategies

Consumer Behavior and Market Research

Digital Marketing and Social Media Strategies

Brand Management and Product Development

Sales Management and Negotiation Techniques

Advertising and Promotional Strategies

International Marketing and Global Markets

Marketing Analytics and Data-Driven Decision Making

Digital Marketing Specialist: Focuses on online marketing strategies, including SEO, PPC, social media, and content marketing, to enhance brand visibility and engagement in the digital space.

Sales Manager: Leads a sales team, developing sales strategies, setting targets, and analyzing sales performance to achieve revenue goals and improve customer relationships.

Advertising Coordinator: Plans and executes advertising campaigns across various media channels, collaborating with creative teams to develop compelling content that resonates with target audiences.

Product Marketing Specialist: Works closely with product development teams to create marketing strategies for new products, ensuring alignment with market needs and customer expectations.

Content Marketing Strategist: Develops and manages content marketing initiatives, creating valuable content that attracts and engages target audiences, ultimately driving customer action.
