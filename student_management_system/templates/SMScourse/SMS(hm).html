BACHELOR OF SCIENCE IN HOSPITALITY MANAGEMENT (BSHM) / ASSOCIATE IN HOSPITALITY MANAGEMENT

The Bachelor of Science in Hospitality Management (BSHM) program, along with the Associate in Hospitality Management, is designed to provide students with a comprehensive understanding of the hospitality industry, equipping them with the skills and knowledge necessary for a successful career in various sectors such as hotels, restaurants, event planning, and tourism. The program emphasizes both theoretical concepts and practical applications, focusing on areas such as customer service, operations management, and hospitality marketing. Students gain hands-on experience through internships, industry projects, and capstone experiences.

Key Areas of Study:

Principles of Hospitality Management

Food and Beverage Management

Hotel and Lodging Operations

Event Planning and Management

Customer Service Excellence

Hospitality Marketing and Sales

Financial Management in Hospitality

Sustainable Practices in Hospitality

Primary Job Role:

Hotel Manager: Oversees the daily operations of a hotel, ensuring guest satisfaction, managing staff, and optimizing financial performance.

Restaurant Manager: Responsible for the overall operation of a restaurant, including staff management, customer service, and financial performance.

Event Coordinator: Plans and executes events such as weddings, conferences, and corporate meetings, managing logistics, budgets, and client relationships.

Front Office Manager: Manages the front desk operations of a hotel, ensuring efficient check-in/check-out processes and addressing guest inquiries and concerns.

Food and Beverage Director: Oversees all food and beverage operations within a hospitality establishment, ensuring quality service and compliance with health regulations.

Sales and Marketing Manager: Develops and implements marketing strategies to promote hospitality services, driving customer engagement and revenue growth.

Guest Services Manager: Focuses on enhancing the guest experience by managing customer service teams and implementing service standards.

Tourism Development Officer: Works with local governments and organizations to promote tourism, develop attractions, and enhance visitor experiences in a region.
