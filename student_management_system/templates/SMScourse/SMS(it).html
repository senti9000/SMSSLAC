BACHELOR OF SCIENCE IN COMPUTER SCIENCE (BSCS)

The Bachelor of Science in Computer Science (BSCS) program is designed to provide students with a strong foundation in computing, equipping them with the skills and knowledge necessary for a career in software development, systems analysis, and more. The program emphasizes both theoretical and practical approaches, focusing on areas such as algorithms, software development, and emerging technologies. Students gain hands-on experience through industry-aligned internships and capstone projects.

Key Areas of Study:

Algorithmic foundations and problem-solving techniques

Software design and development

Database management and data processing

Web development and programming

Network administration and cybersecurity

Primary Job Role:

Software Engineer: Designs, develops, tests, and maintains software applications, creating efficient, scalable solutions to meet user needs and business goals.

System Analyst: Analyzes an organizations technology needs, recommending and implementing software solutions that improve operations and meet business requirements.

Software Developer: Specializes in building, testing, and deploying applications for end users, focusing on functionality, user experience, and performance.

Data Analyst: Interprets data to uncover insights, creating reports and visualizations to support strategic decision-making within the organization.

Development Professional: Innovates and develops new technologies, products, or processes, advancing industry capabilities and improving company offerings.

Assurance Specialist: Ensures software and applications meet quality standards through rigorous testing, identifying bugs and verifying that systems function as expected.

Programmer: Writes, tests, and maintains the code that allows software programs to function, ensuring reliability and performance of applications.

Developer: Designs and builds websites, creating responsive, user-friendly web interfaces and ensuring optimal functionality and accessibility across devices.

BACHELOR OF SCIENCE IN COMPUTER ENGINEERING (BSCpE)

The Bachelor of Science in Computer Engineering (BSCE) program is designed to provide students with a comprehensive understanding of both computer science and electrical engineering principles. This interdisciplinary program equips students with the skills and knowledge necessary for a career in hardware and software integration, embedded systems, and networked systems. The curriculum emphasizes both theoretical foundations and practical applications, focusing on areas such as circuit design, microprocessors, and system architecture. Students gain hands-on experience through laboratory work, industry-aligned internships, and capstone projects.

Key Areas of Study:

Digital and analog circuit design

Microprocessor architecture and programming

Embedded systems and real-time computing

Computer networks and communication systems

Software engineering and application development

Signal processing and control systems

Primary Job Role:

Hardware Engineer:Designs and develops computer hardware components, including circuit boards, processors, and memory devices, ensuring optimal performance and reliability.

Embedded Systems Engineer: Specializes in designing and programming embedded systems, integrating hardware and software to create efficient, real-time applications for various devices.

Network Engineer: Plans, implements, and manages computer networks, ensuring secure and efficient communication between devices and systems.

Systems Architect: Develops the overall architecture of computer systems, ensuring that hardware and software components work together seamlessly to meet organizational needs.

Firmware Engineer: Writes and tests low-level software that directly interacts with hardware components, ensuring proper functionality and performance of devices.

Test Engineer: Conducts rigorous testing of hardware and software systems to identify defects, ensuring that products meet quality standards and specifications.

Field Application Engineer: Works directly with customers to provide technical support and solutions, helping to integrate and optimize hardware and software products in real-world applications.

Research and Development Engineer: Innovates and develops new technologies, products, or processes, advancing industry capabilities and improving company offerings in the field of computer engineering.

BACHELOR OF SCIENCE IN INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY (BSIT)

The Bachelor of Science in Information Technology (BSIT) program is designed to provide students with a comprehensive understanding of information systems, technology infrastructure, and the application of technology in business environments. This program equips students with the skills and knowledge necessary for a career in IT management, system administration, and network security. The curriculum emphasizes both theoretical concepts and practical applications, focusing on areas such as system design, database management, and cybersecurity. Students gain hands-on experience through real-world projects, internships, and collaborative learning.

Key Areas of Study:

Information systems analysis and design

Network architecture and administration

Database management and data analytics

Web development and application programming

Cybersecurity principles and practices

IT project management and strategy

Primary Job Role:

IT Manager: Oversees the IT department, managing technology resources and ensuring that IT strategies align with organizational goals.

System Administrator: Manages and maintains an organization’s IT infrastructure, ensuring the availability, performance, and security of systems and networks.

Network Engineer: Designs, implements, and manages network solutions, ensuring efficient and secure communication between devices and systems.

Database Administrator: Responsible for the performance, integrity, and security of databases, managing data storage and retrieval processes.

Web Developer: Designs and develops websites and web applications, focusing on user experience, functionality, and performance.

Cybersecurity Analyst: Protects an organization’s information systems from cyber threats, implementing security measures and monitoring for vulnerabilities.

IT Support Specialist: Provides technical support and troubleshooting for hardware and software issues, assisting users in resolving technology-related problems.

Business Analyst: Analyzes business needs and technology requirements, recommending solutions that enhance operational efficiency and support strategic objectives.
